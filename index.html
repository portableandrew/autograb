What we're here to do

AutoGrab has the intent and the mandate to build an AI-first engineering organisation. What it needs now is a proven agentic development process, embedded in real delivery conditions on its own codebase, and a team that knows how to sustain it.

Portable will embed two experienced AI practitioners alongside AutoGrab's engineering team. We start by getting inside the organisation — understanding how work gets done, how the team operates, and where the right first opportunity sits. From there we install and run an agentic AI development process on a real project, bring the team along through delivery, and close the engagement with a detailed roadmap for how AutoGrab applies this to every project that follows.

How we work

Explore first. Build on what's real.

We don't arrive with a predetermined answer. The first two weeks are working time — getting inside the organisation, understanding the team, and identifying the right low-risk project to start on.

Phase 01
Explore and identify
Weeks 1–2

Both consultants embed with the AutoGrab engineering team from 13 April. This is working time, not a formal review: meeting the engineers, understanding the current development workflow, and identifying the best low-risk project to pilot the agentic development process on.

By the end of week two we have an agreed pilot scope — a real project, with a real team, ready to run on the new process.

Phase 02
Install the process
Weeks 3–6

Portable leads delivery on the pilot project using a fully agentic AI development process: AI-assisted requirements, design, implementation, code review, and testing. AutoGrab's engineers work alongside throughout.

The goal is a delivered pilot and a team that has experienced the process firsthand — not a team that has been shown slides about it.

Phase 03
Deploy and extend
Weeks 7–9

AutoGrab's engineers take increasing ownership. Portable provides guidance as the process is extended to the broader team and to the next project.

The engagement closes on 14 June 2026 with a detailed roadmap covering precisely how AutoGrab applies the agentic development process to each of its next projects — the specific tools, workflow patterns, and practices, and exactly how to get there.
